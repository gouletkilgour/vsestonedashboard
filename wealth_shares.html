Wealth Shares

About this data

Wealth Shares

A wealth share measures the fraction of all net personal wealth held by a particular group of the population, ranked from poorest to richest. These series focus on the wealth share of key segments of the distribution: the top 1%, the top 10%, and the bottom 50%.

For example, if the top 1%'s share of wealth is 28%, it means that the wealthiest 1% of adults collectively hold 28% of all net personal wealth in Canada.

Note that the top 10% includes the top 1% (it is not an exclusive "next 9%" group), so the two series overlap rather than sum to a whole.

Net Personal Wealth

Net personal wealth = Non-financial assets + Financial assets − Debt, held by households and private individuals (including unincorporated businesses not separated from their owners).

Unit of Analysis

These series use the "equal-split adults" convention: wealth is measured at the individual level (adults aged 20+), but resources are split equally between partners within a couple.

Data source: World Inequality Database (WID.world), variable shweal___992_j, Canada
